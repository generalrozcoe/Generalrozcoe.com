About Me

My name is Erving-Michael Phillip Rivera, but I prefer the name General Rozcoe. For the majority of my life, I have been living in the South Bronx meaning that I have been surrounded in "characters" for the longest. From a young age, my mother did her best to prevent me from being out in the streets to make sure I wouldn't be getting in trouble. She rather I be in the house, playing video games or playing on the computer under her supervision. My middle school was less than 100 yards away and she had me getting picked up until I was 13 years old. My mother was kind of right because when I had that taste of freedom, I started going almost 1/3 of a mile just for a girl. When I got to High School, I would sit in the courtyard with friends until almost 6. At one point, I walked in the door to my mother in tears, 2 minutes away from calling the police. Eventually, I reverted to what my mother wanted. I started gaming on my computer and would get home almost immediately, just to play video games. On my 15th birthday, I spent all of my money on computer parts. That September, I started making YouTube videos with my friends. It wasn't much at all. Of all things we could play, we decided to do Minecraft videos, LIKE EVERY OTHER YOUTUBER. At some point, I got addicted to editing videos. I ventured into playing other video games and tried making whole playthroughs. Did not end up successfully. I started taking my mother's cameras and recorded everything I saw. I began recording family gatherings and was happier with my larger pool of available footage. I recorded my aunts retirement and tried selling DVD's of the events but my mother's camera couldn't handle the darkness. I invested into purchasing my own camera which was a Canon T3I and I started to delve deeper into the film world. I took this camera everywhere and took as many pictures and videos I could. I took a temporary hiatus from my second semester of Junior year of high school into my first semester of my Senior year. As I was preparing for college applications, I decided I wanted to major in film. As I was developing my portfolio, I started to uncover more and more of my work and decided to rekindle my fire for film and photography. Making this website to display my work is what I considered my next major step.
